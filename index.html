The Possible Outcomes

The answer is not always “build an app.”

A responsible technology decision may lead to one of six directions. The goal is to choose the least complicated path that reliably serves the business.

01

Buy

Use an existing product because the business need is standard and already solved effectively.

02

Connect

Keep the current tools but integrate them into a more coherent operating workflow.

03

Repair

Complete or stabilize an application that has already been started.

04

Extend

Remain on the existing platform while adding integrations, architecture, or custom functionality.

05

Build

Create proprietary technology because the process is differentiated, valuable, and poorly served by existing tools.

06

Wait

Delay the investment until the process, demand, offer, or expected return is better validated.

What Happens During the Fit Check?

A short conversation to determine whether a deeper assessment is warranted.

Fifteen minutes is not enough to design your entire system. It is enough to identify whether there is a meaningful business and technology problem, whether my approach fits it, and what the appropriate next step should be.

01

You explain the business, its customer, and the process you are trying to improve or preserve.

02

We identify the visible friction in the current tools, workflow, or unfinished application.

03

We discuss whether the likely direction is to buy, connect, repair, extend, build, or wait.

04

We determine whether the business qualifies for a complete Technology Ownership Assessment.

05

If there is a fit, I explain the assessment and next phase; if not, I give you the most responsible direction I can identify.
